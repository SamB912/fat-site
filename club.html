First and Third Trinity Boat Club is the boat club of Trinity College in Cambridge, UK. Trinity is the largest of the Cambridge University Colleges. The club typically teaches between 60 and 80 people to learn to row each year. We cater for everyone - boats will train anywhere from twice to 12 times a week. We have up to 14 boats rowing in the Cambridge May Bumps - the highlight of the College rowing calendar.

Rowing at Trinity started with the formation of First Trinity Boat Club in 1825. Second and Third Trinity were formed shortly after this. All three clubs enjoyed a lot of success during the 19th century. The dissolution of Second Trinity did nothing to dim the college's success. The Clubs were a formidable force, sending crews to the Olympics, and winning the Grand at Henley several times.

In the early 20th century, First Trinity BC and Third Trinity BC remained separate entities. The latter was only open to students coming up from Eton or Westminster. The college rowing scene flourished, and many college boat clubs grew in membership. At the same time, Third Trinity suffered from a decreasing number appropriate students. To remain competitive with these larger clubs, the two Trinity clubs merged in 1947. The new club, First and Third Trinity Boat Club, is still one of the largest and most successful on the Cam.
